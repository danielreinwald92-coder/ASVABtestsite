Ratios and Proportions

The most frequently tested topic on the AR section.

The Cross-Multiply Method

Set up a proportion with matching units on both sides.

a/b = c/d → a × d = b × c
Example: If 3 apples cost $2, how much do 12 apples cost?
3/2 = 12/x → 3x = 24 → x = $8

Percentages

Common in real-world scenarios like discounts, tax, and interest.

Basic Formula

Part = Whole × (Percent/100)

Percent Change

New = Original × (1 ± percent/100)
Discount: $50 with 20% off = $50 × 0.80 = $40
Warning: When finding the original after a % increase, divide! If X + 20% = $54,000, then X = $54,000 ÷ 1.20 = $45,000

Distance, Rate, Time

D = R × T   |   R = D/T   |   T = D/R

Toward each other: ADD speeds
Same direction: SUBTRACT speeds

Critical: Average speed ≠ average of speeds! Use Total Distance ÷ Total Time.
Ratios Easy

If a car travels 150 miles on 6 gallons of gas, how many gallons are needed to travel 400 miles?

  • A 14 gallons
  • B 16 gallons
  • C 18 gallons
  • D 20 gallons

Solution

  1. Set up proportion: 150/6 = 400/x
  2. Cross multiply: 150x = 2,400
  3. Solve: x = 16 gallons
Verify: 150÷6 = 25 mpg. 400÷25 = 16 ✓
Percentages Medium

A jacket priced at $80 is 25% off. With 8% sales tax, what's the final price?

  • A $60.00
  • B $64.80
  • C $68.40
  • D $72.00

Solution

  1. Discount: $80 × 0.75 = $60
  2. Tax: $60 × 1.08 = $64.80
Shortcut: $80 × 0.75 × 1.08 = $64.80
Distance/Rate/Time Medium

Two cars start from the same point traveling opposite directions at 55 mph and 45 mph. When will they be 300 miles apart?

  • A 2 hours
  • B 3 hours
  • C 4 hours
  • D 5 hours

Solution

  1. Opposite directions: ADD speeds = 100 mph
  2. Time = 300 ÷ 100 = 3 hours

Pythagorean Theorem

The most frequently tested geometry concept.

a² + b² = c² (c is always the longest side)
Example: Right triangle with legs 6 and 8.
6² + 8² = 36 + 64 = 100 → c = 10
(Or recognize: 3-4-5 × 2 = 6-8-10)

FOIL Method

For multiplying binomials: First, Outer, Inner, Last

(x + 3)(x - 5)
First: x² | Outer: -5x | Inner: 3x | Last: -15
= x² - 2x - 15

Exponent Rules

Same base multiplication: ADD exponents

x³ × x⁴ = x⁷

Power of a power: MULTIPLY exponents

(x³)² = x⁶

Inequalities

Critical rule: FLIP THE SIGN when multiplying or dividing by a negative!
Example: -3x > 9
Divide by -3 (flip!): x < -3
Linear Equations Easy

Solve: 5x - 12 = 3x + 8

  • A x = 2
  • B x = 5
  • C x = 10
  • D x = 20

Solution

  1. Subtract 3x: 2x - 12 = 8
  2. Add 12: 2x = 20
  3. Divide: x = 10
Pythagorean Theorem Medium

A right triangle has legs of 6 and 8. What is the hypotenuse?

  • A 7
  • B 10
  • C 12
  • D 14

Solution

  1. 6² + 8² = 36 + 64 = 100
  2. √100 = 10
Shortcut: 3-4-5 triple × 2 = 6-8-10
Inequalities Hard

Solve: -3x + 7 > 16

  • A x > 3
  • B x < 3
  • C x > -3
  • D x < -3

Solution

  1. Subtract 7: -3x > 9
  2. Divide by -3 (FLIP!): x < -3

Vocabulary Easy

ABANDON most nearly means

  • A keep
  • B desert
  • C find
  • D protect

Solution

Desert means to leave behind — same as abandon.

Think: An "abandoned building" is one that's been deserted.
Vocabulary Medium

BENEVOLENT most nearly means

  • A mean
  • B violent
  • C kind
  • D weak

Solution

Benevolent means kind and charitable.

Word parts: "bene" = good + "volent" = wishing = good-wishing = kind!
Vocabulary Hard

IMPEDE most nearly means

  • A help
  • B speed up
  • C block
  • D move

Solution

Impede means to obstruct or hinder progress.

An "impediment" is an obstacle — something that impedes.

Paragraph Comprehension

Reading comprehension — understanding passages and answering questions about what you read.

11
Questions
27
Minutes
~2.5
Min/Question

Key Strategies

  • Read the question first — Know what to look for before reading the passage.
  • Stick to the text — Answers must be supported by the passage. No outside knowledge.
  • Find evidence — Point to specific words that support your answer.
  • Go back and verify — You have time. Re-read before choosing.

Elimination Rules

Usually Wrong
  • "always", "never", "all"
  • Too narrow or too broad
  • Not stated in text
Often Correct
  • "some", "may", "often"
  • Matches passage scope
  • Traceable to text

Question Types

  • Main Idea — "What is the passage primarily about?" Look at first and last sentences.
  • Supporting Details — "According to the passage..." The answer IS directly stated.
  • Inference — "It can be inferred..." Not stated, but logically follows from text.
  • Vocabulary in Context — Substitute each answer choice into the sentence.
  • Author's Tone — Look at word choices. Positive, negative, or neutral?
Main Idea Easy
The honeybee is one of the most important insects on Earth. Without bees, many of the foods we eat would not exist. Bees pollinate approximately 80% of flowering plants, including many fruits and vegetables. When a bee visits a flower to collect nectar, pollen sticks to its body and transfers to the next flower.

What is the main idea of this passage?

  • A Bees collect nectar from flowers
  • B Bees are essential for pollinating plants we depend on
  • C Pollen sticks to bees when they visit flowers
  • D 80% of plants are flowering plants

Solution

The passage emphasizes the importance of bees for pollination and food production.

A, C, and D are details, not the main idea. Main idea = the BIG picture.
Inference Medium
The development of penicillin is often credited to Alexander Fleming, who discovered the antibiotic properties of the Penicillium mold in 1928. However, Fleming was unable to produce penicillin in large quantities. It was not until 1941 that Howard Florey and Ernst Boris Chain developed methods for mass production, making the drug available to treat wounded soldiers during World War II.

What can be inferred about penicillin before 1941?

  • A It was widely used to treat infections
  • B It was available only in limited quantities
  • C Alexander Fleming refused to share his discovery
  • D World War II soldiers did not need antibiotics

Solution

The passage states Fleming "was unable to produce penicillin in large quantities" — implying limited availability before 1941.
